When you first log in as a shop owner, a **Shop Setup** panel appears in the bottom-right corner of the screen. It tracks 16 setup steps and shows your progress as a percentage. You don't have to complete them in order, and you can minimize the panel at any time using the **×** in the top corner — it won't disappear permanently until all 16 steps are done.

## The 16 steps

### 1. Set your shop name & info
**Where:** Settings → Booking Page

Add your shop's name and address. Both appear on your public booking page so clients know where to find you before they book.

### 2. Upload your shop logo
**Where:** Settings → Booking Page

Your logo appears on the public booking page and in client-facing confirmation emails. Accepted formats: PNG, JPG, WebP. Click **Upload Logo** and select your file.

### 3. Set your cancellation policy
**Where:** Settings → Booking Page

Enable the cancellation policy toggle and write your policy text. Clients must acknowledge it before confirming a booking online. A clear policy gives you documented ground to stand on for no-shows and late cancellations.

### 4. Add your services
**Where:** Settings → Services

Add at least one service (e.g. Haircut, Beard Trim). Each service needs a name, duration, and price. Clients pick from this list when booking. See [Adding Services & Pricing](/help/getting-started/services-pricing) for a full walkthrough.

### 5. Set your tax rate
**Where:** Settings → Tax Rates

Enter your local sales tax rate for products and/or services. Check with your local tax authority for the correct rate — most states don't tax services, but do tax retail products.

### 6. Set your shop hours
**Where:** Settings → Shop Hours

Toggle each day of the week on or off and set open/close times. These control when the online booking page shows availability to clients. Click **Save Hours** when done.

After saving, click **Mark as done ✓** on this step in the checklist — shop hours require manual confirmation.

### 7. Set your minimum booking notice
**Where:** Settings → Booking Page

This is how far in advance a client must book. For example, a 30-minute minimum means clients can't book a slot that starts in the next 30 minutes. This prevents last-second bookings while you're mid-appointment.

Click **Mark as done ✓** after you've set this.

### 8. Configure your booking window
**Where:** Settings → Booking Page

This controls how far ahead clients can book online. The default is 60 days. A shorter window keeps your calendar manageable; a longer window is useful if clients like to plan ahead.

Click **Mark as done ✓** after setting it.

### 9. Add your barbers
**Where:** Settings → Barbers

Add each member of your team. See [Adding Barbers & Staff](/help/getting-started/adding-barbers) for the full walkthrough. This step marks itself complete automatically once you have more than one barber on the account.

### 10. Set barber commission rates
**Where:** Settings → Barbers

Each barber needs a **Service Commission Rate** (what percentage of each service they earn) and a **Product Commission Rate**. These feed directly into payroll calculations. Click **Edit** on each barber to set their rates.

### 11. Customize your booking page URL
**Where:** Settings → Booking Page → Customize URL slug

Your booking page is automatically assigned a URL like `standtallbooking.com/book/your-shop-name-a6adc896`. Customize the slug (the part after `/book/`) to match your shop name and remove the auto-generated suffix. See [Your Public Booking Page URL](/help/getting-started/booking-page-url).

### 12. Add your social media links
**Where:** Settings → Booking Page → Social Links

Turn on the toggle next to Instagram, Facebook, or TikTok and paste your profile URL. These appear on your public booking page.

Click **Mark as done ✓** when finished.

### 13. Connect Stripe to accept payments
**Where:** Settings → Payments

Click **Connect with Stripe** and follow the prompts to create or link a Stripe account. Stripe is required to collect deposits through the booking page. It's free to sign up.

### 14. Enable deposits
**Where:** Settings → Payments

Once Stripe is connected, you can require clients to pay a deposit at the time of booking. Set the deposit percentage and whether tips are collected pre-tip. Deposits are refunded or forfeited based on the cancellation policy you set in step 3.

*(Stripe must be connected first.)*

### 15. Import your existing clients
**Where:** Clients → Import

If you're coming from Vagaro, Square, or another platform, you can import your client list via CSV. Click **Mark as done ✓** once you've imported or decided to skip this.

### 16. Share your booking link
**Where:** Settings → Booking Page

Copy your booking link and share it — Instagram bio, Google Business profile, your website, or a direct text to clients. Once this step is marked done, the checklist closes and you'll see a **"You're all set! 🎉"** confirmation.

## How steps are marked complete

Some steps mark themselves as complete automatically (for example, "Add your services" completes the moment you save your first service). Others require you to click **Mark as done ✓** because Stand Tall can't automatically detect when you've finished them (like reviewing your hours or sharing your link). The checklist shows which is which.
