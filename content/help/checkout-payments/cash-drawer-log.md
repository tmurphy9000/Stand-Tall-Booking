The Cash Drawer Log is a running record of every cash movement at your shop — money in from checkouts, manual deposits, and withdrawals. It keeps your cash on hand accurate without needing a separate spreadsheet.

![Cash Drawer Log section showing entries with running balance](/help-assets/screenshots/checkout/cash-drawer-log.png)

## Where to find it

The Cash Drawer Log appears in two places:

- **Transactions page** — at the bottom of the page, below the transaction table. Filtered by the same date range selected above.
- **Cash Tracker page** — a standalone view accessible from the sidebar, showing all cash activity with a search and type filter.

## What gets logged automatically

Every time a checkout is completed with **Cash** (or **Other**) as the payment method, the amount is added to the cash drawer log automatically. The entry shows the date, time, barber name, and a note referencing the client name and service.

## Adding a manual entry

Use these buttons to log cash movements that happen outside of checkouts:

- **Add Cash In** — use this for starting cash at the beginning of a day, tips paid in cash separately, or any other money coming in.
- **Log Withdrawal** — use this when taking cash out for change, petty cash, end-of-day drops, or any other reason.

Both forms ask for:
- **Amount** — the dollar amount moving in or out
- **Employee** — which staff member the entry is attributed to
- **Note** (optional) — a short description

## Reading the Cash Drawer Log

| Column | What it shows |
|---|---|
| **Date / Time** | When the entry was recorded |
| **Employee** | Staff member the entry is attributed to |
| **Type** | **Deposit** (money in, shown in green) or **Withdrawal** (money out, shown in amber) |
| **Amount** | Dollar amount, with + or − prefix |
| **Note** | Description of the entry |
| **Running Balance** | The cumulative cash on hand at that moment, accounting for all entries up to that point |

The **Cash on Hand** tile at the top of the Transactions page shows the all-time net balance — total cash in minus total cash out, across all time, not just the selected date range.

## Exporting the cash log

Click the **Export** button in the Cash Drawer Log section header to download a CSV file with all entries visible in the current date filter. The file includes the running balance column.

## End-of-day reconciliation

To verify your drawer at close:

1. Filter Transactions to **Today**.
2. Scroll to the Cash Drawer Log.
3. The **Running Balance** on the last entry shows what should be in your drawer.
4. Count the physical cash and compare. Any difference typically points to a missed withdrawal log or a checkout recorded under the wrong payment method.
